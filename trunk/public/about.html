What is the PullaClub.com?

PullaClub.com is an institution. A social activity and network to cherish and maintain the tradition of a weekly get-together with Pulla. Window stickers and T-Shirts are available for those wishing to promote PullaClub.com.

How do I join PullaClub.com?

Fill the application form. We'll handle your application, and if the applicant is deemed worthy we will invite you to an initiation ceremony.

A recommendation from a senior member is mandatory for all applicants.

Payments

Each PullaClub.com member is to pay a monthly fee of 3 euros. This 3 euros fee goes towards both pulla purchases and site hosting fees. This fee may be reduced over time, dpending on the amount of future members. Members who do not pay their monthly fee within the calendar month will have their club membership withdrawn.

What is Pulla?

Pulla is sweet-tasting bread, typically eaten with coffee, tea, hot chocolate or, in some cases, milk - and always in good company.

How do I bake Pulla?

Ingredients
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cups warm water (45 degrees C)
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 9 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
Put the milk in a small saucepan and bring it to a simmer (heat it until a ring of small bubbles is visible around the sides of the pan). Remove the pan from the heat and cool the milk to a room temperature.

In a large bowl, whisk the yeast into the warm water. Set aside for five minutes, or until the yeast has dissolved and is creamy. Whisk in the milk, sugar, cardamom, salt and eggs. Switch to a wooden spoon, add 2 cups of flour, and beat the mixture until smooth. Beat in the butter and add as much additional flour, 1/2 cup at a time, as you can until the dough is stiff but not dry.

Cover the bowl with cling-film (plastic wrapping) and allow the dough to rest for 15 minutes.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead until it is smooth and satiny, about 10 minutes.

Shape the dough into a ball. Place it in a lightly greased bowl, turn it around in the bowl to grease the top, and cover the bowl tightly with cling-film. Let the dough rise at room temperature until it doubles in bulk, 45 minutes to 1 hour.

Line a large baking sheet with grease-proof paper and set aside.

Turn the dough out onto the oiled surface and knead it lightly and briefly, just to deflate it and release the air. Divide the dough into thirds and roll each third into a rope of about 36 inches long. Braid the three strands, braiding as far down to the bottom of the strands as you can. Lift the long braid onto the paper-lined baking tray, shaping it into a circle as you place it on the pan. Snip about 1 inch of dough off each end of the braid and fuse the ends together, pressing ans pinching them (if necessary) to fit.

Cover the wreath with a kitchen towel and allow it to rise at room temperature until it is puffy but not doubled, about 45 minutes.

Center a rack in the oven and preheat the oven to 190 degrees C.

Brush the egg glaze over the bread. Sprinkle the wreath with sliced almonds and pearl sugar.

Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until golden, taking care not to over bake the wreath. Transfer the loaf to a rack to cool at room temperature before cutting.

Storing: The bread will keep for a day at room temperature, lightly covered with plastic. If you want to keep the bread longer, wrap it air tight ans freeze it for up to 1 month. Thaw, still wrapped, at room temperature.
