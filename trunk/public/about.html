What is the PullaClub.com?

PullaClub.com is an institution. A social activity and network to cherish and maintain the tradition of a weekly get-together with Pulla. Window stickers and T-Shirts are available for those wishing to promote PullaClub.com.

How do I join PullaClub.com?

Fill the application form. We'll handle your application, and if the applicant is deemed worthy we will invite you to an initiation ceremony.

A recommendation from a senior member is mandatory for all applicants.

Payments

Each PullaClub.com member is to pay a monthly fee of 3 euros. This 3 euros fee goes towards both pulla purchases and site hosting fees. This fee may be reduced over time, dpending on the amount of future members. Members who do not pay their monthly fee within the calendar month will have their club membership withdrawn.

What is Pulla?

Pulla is sweet-tasting bread, typically eaten with coffee, tea, hot chocolate or, in some cases, milk - and always in good company.

How do I bake Pulla?

Recipe for 20-24 pieces.
Ingredients
  • 5 dl milk
  • 50 g package yeast
  • 950 g wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 dl white sugar
  • 200 g melted butter (unsalted)
  • 1 egg for glaze, beaten
  • pearl sugar and/or almond slices for topping

Warm milk to 40° C. In a large bowl, add yeast, salt, sugar and cardamom.

Add half of the flour and knead until dough is stiff but not dry.

Cover the bowl (e.g. with plastic-wrapping) and put in warm place. Allow the dough to rest for 15 minutes or until it has doubled in size..

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead until it is smooth and satiny, about 10 minutes.

Shape the dough into a ball. Place it in a lightly greased bowl, turn it around in the bowl to grease the top, and cover the bowl tightly with cling-film. Let the dough rise at room temperature until it doubles in bulk, 45 minutes to 1 hour.

Line a large baking sheet with grease-proof paper and set aside.

Turn the dough out onto the oiled surface and knead it lightly and briefly, just to deflate it and release the air. Divide the dough into small balls (Pullas) and put them on the baking sheet.

Cover the Pullas with a kitchen towel and allow it to rise at room temperature until it is puffy but not doubled, about 45 minutes.

Center a rack in the oven and preheat the oven to 200° C.

Brush the egg glaze over the bread. Sprinkle the wreath with sliced almonds and pearl sugar.

Bake for 12-15 minutes, until golden, taking care not to over bake. Transfer the Pullas to cool at room temperature before cutting.

Storing: Pullas will keep for a day at room temperature, lightly covered with plastic. If you want to keep longer, wrap it air tight ans freeze it for up to 1 month. Thaw, still wrapped, at room temperature.
